Career Guidance Center

Expert advice and resources to help you navigate your career journey

Resume & CV Tips

Keep It Concise

Limit your resume to 1-2 pages. Focus on relevant experience and achievements that match the job description.

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with powerful verbs like "Led," "Developed," "Achieved," or "Implemented" to showcase your impact.

Quantify Achievements

Include numbers and metrics wherever possible. Example: "Increased sales by 35%" or "Managed team of 12."

Tailor for Each Job

Customize your resume for every application by highlighting the most relevant skills and experiences.

Interview Preparation

1

Research the Company

Learn about the company's mission, values, recent news, and culture. Visit their website, social media, and read employee reviews.

2

Practice Common Questions

Prepare answers for questions like "Tell me about yourself," "What are your strengths?" and "Why do you want this job?"

3

Prepare Your Own Questions

Have 3-5 thoughtful questions ready about the role, team dynamics, company goals, and growth opportunities.

4

Dress Appropriately

Choose professional attire that matches the company culture. When in doubt, it's better to be slightly overdressed.

5

Follow Up

Send a thank-you email within 24 hours, expressing gratitude and reiterating your interest in the position.

Career Development

Continuous Learning

Stay updated with industry trends through online courses, certifications, workshops, and professional development programs.

Build Your Network

Attend industry events, join professional associations, and connect with peers on LinkedIn to expand your opportunities.

Set Clear Goals

Define short-term and long-term career objectives. Review and adjust them regularly to stay on track.

Seek Mentorship

Find experienced professionals who can provide guidance, advice, and insights into your career path.

Job Search Strategies

Optimize Your Online Presence

Create a professional LinkedIn profile, clean up social media, and consider building a personal website or portfolio.

Use Multiple Job Boards

Don't rely on just one platform. Explore EasyJobs, LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, and industry-specific sites.

Set Up Job Alerts

Create alerts for your target positions so you're notified immediately when new opportunities arise.

Apply Directly to Companies

Research companies you'd like to work for and apply directly through their career pages.

Track Your Applications

Keep a spreadsheet of jobs you've applied to, including dates, contacts, and follow-up actions.

Salary Negotiation Tips

Do Your Research: Know the market rate for your position and experience level using salary surveys and tools.
Wait for the Right Time: Let the employer bring up salary first, ideally after they've made an offer.
Consider the Full Package: Look beyond base salary at benefits, bonuses, stock options, vacation time, and work flexibility.
Be Confident but Flexible: Know your worth, but be willing to negotiate and find common ground.
Get It in Writing: Once you've agreed on terms, request a written offer letter with all details included.
